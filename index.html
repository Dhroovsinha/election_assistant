    Election Timeline & Phases

    Phase 1
    Voter Registration & Roll Revision
    Citizens register to vote or update existing registrations. The Electoral Roll is published and verified. Corrections can be submitted to the ERO. Typically closes 4–6 weeks before election day.
    ✅ Registration Open
    Phase 2
    Election Announcement & Campaigning
    The Model Code of Conduct comes into effect. Candidates file nominations, parties campaign across constituencies. Campaigning officially ends 48 hours before polling day (silent period).
    📢 Campaigning Phase
    Phase 3
    Silent Period (48 Hours Before Voting)
    No campaigning, rallies, or exit polls. A time to reflect and make your final decision. Verify your polling booth location and prepare your documents.
    🔇 Silent Period
    Phase 4
    Voting Day 🗳️
    Polls open from 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM (varies by constituency). Bring your voter ID. Best times to vote: early morning (7–9 AM) or mid-afternoon (2–4 PM) to avoid peak queues. Your ink-marked finger is your badge of honour.
    ⏳ Upcoming
    Phase 5
    Vote Counting & Results
    EVMs are unsealed and votes counted at counting centres. Results are announced constituency by constituency. The Election Commission declares winners. Usually 2–5 days after the final phase of voting.
    📊 Counting & Results
    Phase 6
    Government Formation
    The winning party or coalition forms the government. The President / Governor invites the leader with majority support to form government. New ministers are sworn in and Parliament / Assembly convenes.
    🏛️ Government Formation
